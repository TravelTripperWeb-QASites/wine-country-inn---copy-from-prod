Attractions

Discover the many flavours of the Napa Valley

There's no question that here in Napa Valley, it’s all about the grapes. While Napa Valley has long established itself as a cultural attraction, the vast majority of people who come to St Helena and Napa Valley come for the wine.

Vacation Guide

There are so many things to do in Napa Valley, from enjoying the local eateries to visiting St. Helena wineries. Wine tours are easily one of the more sought after things to do in Napa Valley, from the various wineries in the area to wine tastings to the Napa Wine Train. There are plenty of options to experience Napa Valley wine at its finest. For even more recommendations on things to do in St. Helena, CA, be sure to download a free Vacation Guide.

Napa Valley wine tasting experiences

There are currently more than 500 wineries in Napa Valley and several kinds of Napa Valley wine tours! Napa Valley is filled with amazing wineries from small labels to world-famous names. Before any first visit, it is important to get some background education about “vine to wine”, history, architecture, scenery, views, and caves. Many of the best wineries are located right in St. Helena, and conveniently located near the Wine Country Inn & Cottages. The Wine Country Inn & Cottages guest service staff will offer advice to find the perfect wineries for tasting experiences.

Things to do right on our doorstep in St Helena

From golf to hiking to some of the best restaurants in the world, there is much to do in the Napa Valley and close by to the Wine Country Inn & Cottages in St. Helena. Be sure to explore St. Helena Chamber of Commerce website at www.sthelena.com or www.visitnapavalley.com for advice about local shopping, wineries, events and activities.

Calistoga – a quaint neighbour, well worth a visit

Located only a short drive away from St. Helena and Wine Country Inn & Cottages, the city of Calistoga is the perfect place to visit during a stay. There are a number of great things to do in Calistoga for anyone visiting the area, from visiting a number of Calistoga wineries to many outdoor activities.
