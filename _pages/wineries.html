Wineries

Discover the varietals of the Napa Valley

There's no question that in the Napa Valley, it’s all about the grapes. While the region has long established itself as a cultural attraction, the vast majority of people who come to St. Helena and Napa Valley come for the wine.

World-famous for its buttery chardonnays, fruity merlots, and rich cabernets, Napa and the surrounding towns in the valley are overflowing with good wine. That said, visits to local wineries, wine tours and rides on the Napa Valley Wine Train are easily some of the most popular - and most recommended - attractions in the region.

Wine tasting in the Napa Valley

With more than 500 wineries, Napa Valley is filled with amazing wineries. From small labels to world-famous names, many of the region’s best winemakers are located right in St. Helena, conveniently near the Wine Country Inn & Cottages. While some wineries require reservations, others do not. Guests are encouraged to research ahead of time to make the most of their Napa getaway.

The heart of wine country

Overlooking several St. Helena vineyards, the Wine Country Inn & Cottages is in the heart of wine country. Here guests can explore everything from local favorites to world-renown wineries in the Napa Valley, using the hotel as their starting point.
